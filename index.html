Factorial Calculator





A factorial is the product of all positive integers less than or equal to a given positive integer. For example, the factorial of 5 (denoted as 5!) is calculated as 5 * 4 * 3 * 2 * 1 = 120.

A factorial can be calculated in two ways: iteratively (using loops) or recursively (where a function calls itself). For example: Iteratively: Start from 1 and multiply by each integer up to the target number. Recursively: The factorial of n is n * factorial(n-1), with a base case of 1! = 1.

The factorial of 0 is defined as 1. This is an essential concept in mathematics and is required for various combinatorics calculations.
